## Key Product Claims

- PrismHR Global is positioned as an embedded, SSO-enabled platform for PEOs and payroll service bureaus.
- Product capabilities mentioned: global payroll, employer of record, contractor management, and cross-border recruiting services.
- The platform is positioned around owned in-country payroll entities, built-in compliance controls, higher accuracy, deeper control, and confidence in client outcomes.
- Market scope stated: 175+ markets.
- Business value emphasized: help providers expand reach, attract more prospects, grow revenue, strengthen client relationships, and reduce operational complexity and risk.

## Visual Notes

- The video uses global-market, compliance, and platform UI imagery.

- No Chicagoland-specific targeting appears in the video. Chicagoland prospecting remains a local GTM requirement layered on top of the broader PrismHR Global value proposition.

## App Implications

- Prospecting is not secondary to relationship management. The app must help identify likely-fit companies and accounts that can benefit from PrismHR Global's global workforce capabilities.
- Chicagoland prospecting should be a first-class v1 capability because the user needs a local target-account engine, not only a CSM relationship tracker.
- Territory prospecting must still obey the relationship-motion rules: Chicago can raise priority, but it cannot override permission, CSM ownership, off-limits rules, or source-confidence requirements.
- Prospecting records should capture why a company may fit the PrismHR Global story: cross-border hiring, contractor intensity, hiring velocity, compliance complexity, global payroll need, EOR relevance, contractor-management relevance, and channel path.
